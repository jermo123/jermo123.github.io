Troubleshooting Guides

Add Gel Colors Gradually: Start by adding a few drops of gel color to the clear gel. Mix thoroughly before adding more to prevent over-saturation.
Mix Thoroughly: Use a clean utensil to blend the color evenly throughout the gel, avoiding streaks.
Test Consistency: Apply a small amount on a test surface to check color vibrancy before decorating the entire cake.
Storage: Store colored gel in airtight containers to maintain color integrity and prevent drying.

Prepare the Surface: Ensure the cake surface is smooth and free from moisture. Wipe with a dry cloth if necessary.
Use a Light Dusting of Powdered Sugar or Cornstarch: This helps the edible image stick without sliding.
Careful Placement: Gently place the image onto the cake, starting from one edge and smoothing out any air bubbles with a clean tool.
Avoid Overhandling: Once placed, avoid touching the image to prevent smudging or peeling.
Storage: Keep decorated cakes refrigerated until serving to maintain image integrity.

Keep Ingredients Cold: Store whipped topping and cakes in the refrigerator until just before application.
Proper Whipping: Whip the topping to the desired consistency. Over-whipping can cause it to become grainy, while under-whipping may not hold shapes well.
Use Piping Tips for Precision: Select the appropriate piping tip for the design you're aiming to create.
Apply in Cool Conditions: Decorate in a cool environment to prevent the topping from melting during application.
Stabilize if Needed: Consider using a stabilizer or mix-in to help the whipped topping hold its shape longer, especially in warmer conditions.

Slow Pouring: Pour the clear gel slowly to minimize air incorporation.
Use a Toothpick: Gently pop any visible air bubbles with a clean toothpick before the gel sets.
Stir Gently: Mix the gel slowly and carefully to prevent introducing air.
Temperature Management: Apply the gel in a cool environment to reduce the likelihood of bubbles forming as it sets.
Smooth Application Tools: Ensure that tools used to spread the gel are smooth and clean to prevent trapping air.

Proper Whipping: Ensure the topping is whipped to the right consistencyâ€”firm but smooth.
Use the Right Tools: Utilize clean, chilled spatulas and smoothing tools for an even application.
Apply in Layers: Start by applying a thin layer to seal the cake, then add the whipped topping for a smooth finish.
Temperature Control: Keep the cake and toppings cold during application to prevent melting and uneven spreading.
Practice Consistency: Practice steady, even strokes when spreading the whipped topping to avoid streaks.

Rose Flowers:
Technique: Pipe tightly spiraled petals starting from the center and gradually spacing them outward.
Detailing: Use a finer tip for smaller petals to add intricate details.
Daisy Flowers:
Technique: Pipe larger, evenly spaced petals radiating from a central point, leaving gaps between petals for a classic daisy look.
Center Highlight: Use a contrasting color or a small dollop of whipped topping to emphasize the flower center.
Sunflowers:
Technique: Combine large petals with a textured center. Pipe larger petals around a densely piped center to mimic sunflower seeds.
Color Variation: Tint the whipped topping with yellow for petals and brown for the center using gel colors.
Tulips:
Technique: Pipe smooth, elongated petals with slight curves to resemble tulip shapes.
Layering: Create multiple layers of petals for a realistic tulip appearance.
Lilies:
Technique: Pipe elongated petals with subtle folds to mimic lily petals.
Staggered Layers: Arrange petals in staggered layers to add depth and dimension.

Maintain Clean Print Heads:
Regular Cleaning: Clean the print heads regularly to prevent ink buildup and blockages. Follow the printer's cleaning instructions carefully.
Avoid Drying: If the printer is not in use for extended periods, run a cleaning cycle to keep the ink flowing smoothly.
Adjust Image Settings:
Resolution: Use high-resolution images (at least 300 DPI) to ensure clarity and detail in the printed edible image.
Color Profiles: Calibrate the printer's color settings to match the desired output. Perform test prints to adjust colors as needed.
Handle Printed Images Carefully:
Dry Completely: Allow printed edible images to dry completely before applying to the cake to prevent smudging.
Avoid Moisture Exposure: Protect printed images from moisture and excessive handling to maintain image quality.
